Who am I?

From a professional perspective, I'm someone who loves to solve data challenges in ways that generate real value. I have diverse work experience, but most recently I've been primarily developing and managing full stack data pipelines for data science and engineering applications.

Career backstory:

I did my bachelor's in structural engineering, and worked as an engineering consultant primarily to federal clients. Here I deployed into post-disaster situations and analyzed the failure of buildings and infrastructure. This involved researching ways to improve resiliency in the building code, representing my clients in proceedings for official building standards, and writing publications, giving presentations, teaching courses, and managing projects.

In 2015 I completed an MBA, with a focus on analytics and information systems. I began working in software development consulting, with clients primarily in public health. Working on data projects, I knew I wanted to explore the craft of data science. I began to develop pipelines that automated the validation of data from ETLs for data warehousing and production update processes.

This work provided me with data wrangling and cleaning experience while improving our ability to deliver on data projects. As a result, I had the opportunity to mentor junior data engineers as we expanded the use of this role. I also worked in data governance, strategy, and analytics, working with agency senior staff to develop requirements and building pipelines with Python and SQL that provided my client's with new and better ways to measure the impact of their agency.

As I grew my skills from data engineering and analytics work, I also studied Data Science and Machine Learning with online coursework and learning platforms. I was able to find opportunities for machine learning applications that provided value to the client and were worthy of new funding. While educating non-technical stakeholders about machine learning capabilities, I brainstormed use-cases for ML products, developed working prototypes, and presented them to agency decision makers, which led to new, funded data science projects and a growing data science team.

While developing a data science presence at a large agency, I enrolled in UC Berkeley's Masters of Information and Data Science (MIDS) program. This both reinforced my foundational skills and gave me instruction in cutting edge data science techniques and applications. In my advanced courses, I've focused on Distributed Computing, Natural Language Processing, and Computer Vision. For more information on some of the projects I've worked on both through work and school, please check out the the other tabs.
